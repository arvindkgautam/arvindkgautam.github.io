Case study · BFSI · multi-country

Multi-country CCaaS migration for a Tier-1 BFSI client

From legacy contact centre infrastructure to a cloud-native, AI-ready platform across five ASEAN markets.

Sector
BFSI : Retail & Wholesale Banking
Geography
Singapore, Indonesia, Thailand, Malaysia, Vietnam
Role
Lead architect & commercial owner
Timeline
14 month engagement
Situation

The client ran a fragmented contact centre estate across five ASEAN countries. Each market sat on a different generation of on-premise infrastructure, with three separate IVR platforms, two CTI integrations, and no unified reporting. Agent productivity benchmarks varied wildly. A regulatory tightening on customer interaction logging was approaching, and the existing stack could not meet it without significant rebuild work in every country.

Complication

The obvious answer, a single cloud platform across all markets, was politically difficult. Each country team had built process knowledge around their existing systems. Indonesia and Vietnam had data residency requirements that ruled out several global cloud vendors. The CIO wanted consolidation; the country COOs wanted continuity. The deal was at risk of becoming a five-country negotiation rather than one transformation.

Approach

Reframed the conversation around a regional reference architecture rather than a platform mandate. The principle: one architectural pattern with country-level deployment flexibility. Built joint workshops with each country COO before the formal procurement cycle began, surfacing local concerns early and letting them shape the design.

Technically, the architecture combined a cloud-native CCaaS core with country-specific edge deployments to meet residency rules, AI-ready connectors for agent assist and routing on day one, and a phased cutover plan that kept each market on its existing platform until parity was proven.

Commercially, structured the contract as a regional master agreement with country call-offs, which let each market commit at its own pace without breaking the consolidated commercial framework.

Outcome

Multi-million-dollar regional contract closed within the original cycle, ahead of the regulatory deadline.

  • Five markets aligned on a single architectural pattern with country-level deployment flexibility
  • AI agent assist and intelligent routing shipped as part of the foundation, not a future phase
  • Regulatory logging requirement met in every market before the deadline
  • Operating model unified across countries, with shared reporting and a regional command structure
  • Reference account for the region, opening downstream deals in adjacent BFSI accounts
What I learned

The architecture was the easy part. The hard work was running the political and commercial design as carefully as the technical design. Country COOs needed to feel like the design listened to them, not like a head office was being imposed. When the regional master agreement structure clicked, the rest moved quickly.

Also confirmed something I had suspected for years: in BFSI, AI capability sells the strategic case but reference architecture closes the deal. Buyers want both the future and the discipline.
